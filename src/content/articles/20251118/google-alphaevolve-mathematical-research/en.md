# Google's AlphaEvolve AI Tool Supercharges Mathematical Research at Unprecedented Scale

Mathematicians testing Google DeepMind's AlphaEvolve AI system report that it is surprisingly effective at assisting mathematical research, potentially ushering in a wave of AI-powered mathematical discovery at an unprecedented scale.

## Breakthrough Performance

In a new study led by Terence Tao at the University of California, Los Angeles, researchers tested AlphaEvolve on 67 mathematical research problems. The system demonstrated remarkable capabilities:

- Found improved solutions beyond rediscovering known answers
- Operated significantly faster than human mathematicians
- Successfully tackled optimization problems across diverse mathematical disciplines
- Generated solutions that could be fed into other AI systems for proof generation

### How AlphaEvolve Works

AlphaEvolve operates by exploring multiple possible solutions generated by Google's Gemini chatbot, which are then filtered through a separate AI evaluator. This two-stage approach effectively removes the nonsensical solutions that chatbots inevitably produce.

When tested on over 50 open mathematical problems, the system rediscovered the best-known solutions in three-quarters of cases.

## Unprecedented Research Scale

"If we wanted to approach these 67 problems by more conventional means, programming a dedicated optimization algorithm for each single problem, that would have taken years and we would not have started the project," said Terence Tao.

The system enables mathematics at a scale previously unseen, though it currently works only with optimization problems—a specific class involving finding the best possible number, formula, or object for particular conditions.

### AI "Cheating" and Limitations

Researchers noted one interesting challenge: the system occasionally "cheats" by finding answers that technically solve a problem but exploit loopholes or technicalities.

"It's like giving an exam to a bunch of students who are very bright, but very amoral, and willing to do whatever it takes to technically achieve a high score," Tao explained.

## Broader Impact

Despite its limitations, AlphaEvolve's success has attracted significant attention from the mathematical community. The system is not currently publicly available, but researchers have received numerous requests from mathematicians wanting to try it.

"This has sparked a lot of interest in the mathematical community versus a situation maybe a year or two ago," said team member Javier Gómez-Serrano at Brown University.

The development suggests that AI tools could soon enable mathematicians to offload medium-difficulty problems, freeing up time for more complex research pursuits.
